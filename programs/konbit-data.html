Konbit Data converts SAKALA's existing 310-youth network across 25 sites into a commercial survey and data collection service. The infrastructure already exists. The youth are already enrolled. The community trust is already built. What Konbit Data adds is a commercial methodology layer (BARSS validation), a certification credential (Certified Community Research Associate, 2-day training), and a client acquisition pipeline.

Why clients pay for this: IDB, World Bank, USAID, and academic institutions currently commission Haiti data collection studies at $50,000 to $200,000 per study, through Port-au-Prince-based consultants who hire temporary enumerators with no community relationship. Konbit Data delivers the same product in 72 hours to 2 weeks, from youth who live in the neighborhoods being studied, at 1/40th the cost. The perception-versus-reality gap dataset -- what youth know exists in their neighborhood versus what infrastructure surveys confirm actually exists -- is a data product those institutions cannot buy anywhere else.

The WASH Pilot: The entry product for institutional clients is a $5,000 neighborhood WASH (water, sanitation, hygiene) atlas for Cap-Haïtien. 60 youth, 720 household surveys, 2 weeks to delivery. SOIL is the convener. DINEPA (Haiti's national water authority), OREPA Nord, and the UN WASH Cluster are the target clients. An equivalent study through foreign consultants costs $50,000-200,000 and takes 6-18 months. Konbit Data delivers it for $5,000 in 2 weeks -- and leaves 60 credentialed youth in the community when it's done.
