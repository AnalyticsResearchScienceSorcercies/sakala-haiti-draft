Plakèt is the financial infrastructure that makes all eight programs work as a single system. Two modules: Plakèt Pay (domestic commerce rails for Haitian vendors) and Plakèt Payroll (workforce payment with structured savings splitting). The tech stack is built and maintained in-house at BARSS LLC. No external hire. No outside contractor.

The 60/25/15 Payroll Split

Every worker in the cooperative system receives wages through Plakèt Payroll. The split is not optional. It is the mechanism by which individual wages become community wealth accumulation.

AllocationInstrumentPurposeNotes
60% — Take-HomeHTG gourdesDaily spending (MonCash wallet)Immediate household use
25% — SavingsUSDCInflation-protected savings (Base blockchain)Accessible via Plakèt wallet
15% — CommunityLò SAKALAGold-backed cooperative equity1 Lò = 1g allocated gold

The Lò SAKALA allocation is the wealth accumulation mechanism. Every paycheck routes 15 percent into the Fon Kominote SAKALA sub-trust for the relevant cooperative enterprise. The worker does not see it leave and does not make a savings decision. It accumulates automatically. At Year 10, the Harvest Box sub-trust alone projects $13.1 million in Lò-denominated gold reserves, distributing $223 per cooperative member per quarter.
