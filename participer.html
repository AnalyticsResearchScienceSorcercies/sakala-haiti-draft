Where every dollar goes

The 40 / 40 / 20 Model

40%

Wages

Direct income to the youth, weighted by certification. A wage that beats the street, paid every year as long as the business runs.

40%

The youth's Gold (Lò)

Converted into allocated gold, held in the community trust and assigned to the youth. An asset that belongs to them and that appreciates, sheltered from the collapse of the gourde.

20%

Reinvestment

Funds the next cohort, the next campus. It's the flywheel that makes your dollar catalytic, not spent just once.

A dollar of conventional aid reaches Haitian hands as just 1 to 12 cents, then disappears. A dollar here is held at about 80 cents by the community, and it leaves behind an asset the youth own. See the transparency.

Every dollar tracked. Every allocation visible.
