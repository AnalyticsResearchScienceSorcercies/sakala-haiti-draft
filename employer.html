For entrepreneurs, owners and organizations

A Partnership That Works for You and for Haiti's Young People.

Do you have farmland, a workshop, a business, a social project, or an income-generating activity? Do you want to be part of the change while gaining motivated, serious, and well-trained support?

Put a young person to work with KONKRET. We select, train, and place young Haitians aged 16 to 20 in organizations like yours. You get a committed workforce, our team follows up on the ground, and you take a direct part in rebuilding the economic fabric of Haiti.

Why Choose KONKRET

What You Gain

01
Trained Young People
Every young person we place has been trained in both technical and people skills before arriving at your door.
02
Direct Impact
Your work moves forward, and a young person builds their future. One gesture, two real results.
03
Ongoing Support
Our team stays present on the ground. You are not alone in managing the onboarding process.
04
A Safe Process
Everything is handled through our cooperative structure. Simple, transparent, and with no surprises.
Who Can Take Part

Our Network of Employers

NGOs, Cooperatives & Community Projects

Mission-driven organizations looking to strengthen their field teams with a committed, trained local workforce.

Schools and Faith-Based Institutions

Schools, training centers, parishes, and faith groups running productive activities or community gardens.

Land and Workshop Owners

Entrepreneurs, landowners, artisans, and shopkeepers who want to grow their activity with structured human support.

Available Profiles

The Young People We Train

Farm Worker
Soil preparation, planting, crop care, and harvest. Trained in market-gardening techniques suited to the North.
Processing Assistant
Sorting, packaging, and processing of farm products. Food hygiene and post-harvest handling.
General Laborer
Maintenance, transport, handling, and logistics support on farms or in workshops.
Apprentice Mason
Support on light construction sites. Introduction to masonry techniques and on-site safety.
Sales Clerk
Running a sales point, customer relations, and basic bookkeeping. A good fit for neighborhood businesses.
Administrative Assistant
Data entry, filing, communication, and coordination for small organizations or offices.
