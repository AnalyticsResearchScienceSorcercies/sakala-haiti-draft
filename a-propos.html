Our Mission

The Land as Engine, the Youth as Force

SAKALA Haiti is a Haitian solidarity-economy organization. Its cooperative KONKRET, launched in 2011 in the North, organizes youth employment as the primary answer to the socio-economic breakdown Haiti is living through. We believe agriculture can be an engine of economic transformation when it is collective, cooperative, and built around jobs for young people.

KONKRÈT is not a project. It is an answer. An answer to discouragement, to unemployment, and to the inner exile of our youth.

Our Model

How It Works

01

A Young Person Signs Up

Initial training and orientation. An assessment of skills and aspirations. Every young person is welcomed with a path of their own.

02

An Employer Offers Work

Land, workshop, business... local farming partners and entrepreneurs open their doors and offer real positions. The diaspora can also hire a young person directly from abroad.

03

KONKRET Connects

Support and follow-up throughout the placement. KONKRET stays present, not only at the start but for the whole length of the journey.

04

The Cooperative Grows

Income, ownership, autonomy. The members of the cooperative build something that belongs to them collectively and for the long term.

Our Values

Why Trust Us

Measurable Local Impact

Every job created is documented. Every young person followed. We work with clear indicators because impact without measure is just rhetoric. Our results are visible in our communities.

Transparency and a Cooperative Model

The cooperative belongs to its members. Decisions are collective. Income is shared by rules that are clear and known to all. No hidden beneficiaries.

Sustainable, Inclusive Agriculture

We grow with the resources available locally. No dependence on imported inputs. No exclusion by gender or origin. The land of Milot can feed and employ.
